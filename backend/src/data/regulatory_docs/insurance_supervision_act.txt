INSURANCE SUPERVISION ACT (ISA) — INSURANCE LICENSE REQUIREMENTS
=================================================================

1. LEGAL BASIS
The Federal Act on the Supervision of Insurance Companies (ISA, SR 961.01)
and the Insurance Supervision Ordinance (ISO, SR 961.011) regulate the
licensing and ongoing supervision of insurance undertakings in Switzerland.

2. SCOPE
Any entity offering insurance products in or from Switzerland requires a
FINMA license. This covers direct insurers (life and non-life), reinsurers,
and insurance groups/conglomerates.

3. INSURANCE CLASSES
FINMA distinguishes between:
- Life insurance (Class A): individual life, group life, unit-linked
- Non-life insurance (Class B): property, liability, health, accident,
  motor vehicle, transport, credit, legal expenses
- Reinsurance (Class C): proportional and non-proportional

4. LEGAL FORM
Insurance companies must be organized as:
- Corporation (AG) under Swiss law, OR
- Cooperative (Genossenschaft)
Foreign insurers may operate via a Swiss branch with authorization.

5. MINIMUM CAPITAL
Capital requirements vary by insurance class:
- Life insurance: CHF 5-20 million (depending on scope)
- Non-life insurance: CHF 3-10 million (depending on classes)
- Reinsurance: CHF 3-10 million
- Captive insurers: reduced requirements may apply
FINMA may require additional capital based on risk profile.

6. SOLVENCY REQUIREMENTS — SWISS SOLVENCY TEST (SST)
- All licensed insurers must comply with the SST
- SST is a risk-based capital adequacy framework
- Target capital = risk-bearing capital must exceed required capital
- Standard model or approved internal model may be used
- SST ratio must be reported annually to FINMA
- SST ratio below 100% triggers supervisory intervention

7. TIED ASSETS (GEBUNDENES VERMOEGEN)
- Insurers must maintain tied assets to cover technical provisions
- Tied assets must be invested according to prudent person principle
- Investment restrictions: quality, diversification, liquidity
- Regular reporting on tied asset composition to FINMA
- Custodian bank required for tied asset safekeeping

8. ORGANIZATIONAL REQUIREMENTS
- Board of directors with adequate insurance expertise
- Executive management with professional qualifications
- Clear organizational structure with function separation
- Effective internal control system (ICS)
- Independent compliance, risk management, and actuarial functions
- Internal audit function

9. FIT AND PROPER REQUIREMENTS
Key persons must:
- Enjoy a good reputation
- Have adequate professional qualifications in insurance
- Offer guarantee of proper business conduct (Gewaehr)
- Submit FINMA personal questionnaires

10. RESPONSIBLE ACTUARY
- Mandatory appointment of a responsible actuary (verantwortlicher Aktuar)
- Must be qualified and approved by FINMA
- Responsibilities: technical provisions, premium calculations,
  solvency assessment, annual actuarial report to FINMA
- Independent from executive management

11. BUSINESS PLAN
The license application must include a comprehensive business plan:
- Insurance classes and products to be offered
- Target markets and distribution channels
- Reinsurance program
- Three-year financial projections
- Risk management strategy
- Investment strategy for tied assets

12. GENERAL AND SPECIAL INSURANCE CONDITIONS (AVB)
- Must be submitted to FINMA for approval (life insurance)
- Non-life: notification duty for AVB
- Consumer protection requirements apply
- Transparency in premium calculation and fee disclosure

13. REINSURANCE
- Adequate reinsurance program mandatory
- Reinsurance contracts must be disclosed to FINMA
- Counterparty risk management for reinsurance
- Concentration limits on reinsurance partners

14. POLICYHOLDER PROTECTION
- Insurance guarantee scheme (for certain life insurance policies)
- Claims handling procedures and deadlines
- Complaints handling process
- Information duties to policyholders

15. AML COMPLIANCE (LIFE INSURANCE)
Life insurance companies are financial intermediaries under AMLA and must:
- Implement AML/KYC procedures for life insurance policies
- Affiliate with an SRO or submit to direct FINMA AML supervision
- Identify beneficial owners of policies above threshold

16. AUDIT REQUIREMENTS
- Annual financial audit by a FINMA-recognized audit firm
- Regulatory audit covering ISA compliance
- Actuarial audit of technical provisions

17. APPLICATION PROCESS
- Submit application via FINMA EHP platform
- Processing time: typically 6-12 months
- Requires complete documentation package
- FINMA may impose conditions or restrictions on the license

18. ONGOING OBLIGATIONS
- Annual SST reporting
- Quarterly financial reporting
- Report material changes to FINMA
- Maintain tied assets and solvency requirements
- Comply with FINMA circulars and insurance-specific guidance
