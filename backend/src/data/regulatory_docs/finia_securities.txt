1. LEGAL BASIS
The Federal Act on Financial Institutions (FinIA, SR 954.1) and the
Financial Institutions Ordinance (FinIO, SR 954.11) govern the licensing
and supervision of securities firms in Switzerland. FinIA replaced the
former Stock Exchange Act (SESTA) provisions on securities dealers.

2. SCOPE
A securities firm (Wertpapierhaus) is an entity that professionally:
- Trades in securities for its own account on a short-term basis (proprietary trading)
- Acts as a market maker
- Trades in securities for client accounts (execution-only or advisory)
- Underwrites securities and offers them publicly (underwriting)
- Creates derivatives and offers them publicly

3. LICENSING REQUIREMENT
Operating as a securities firm without a FINMA license is prohibited
(Art. 41 FinIA). The license requirement applies regardless of whether
the activity is conducted from Switzerland or cross-border.

4. LEGAL FORM
Securities firms must be organized as:
- Corporation (AG)
- Limited partnership with shares (Kommandit-AG)
- Limited liability company (GmbH)
Swiss law entity required; foreign firms need a branch license.

5. MINIMUM CAPITAL
- Minimum share capital: CHF 1.5 million, fully paid-up (Art. 45 FinIA)
- Market makers: higher capital requirements may apply
- Capital adequacy requirements per FinIO Art. 63 ff.
- Risk diversification rules (concentration limits)
- Ongoing own funds requirements based on business volume

6. ORGANIZATIONAL REQUIREMENTS
- Board of directors with adequate expertise in securities business
- Executive management with at least two qualified persons
- Management must be conducted from Switzerland
- Clear separation of functions: front office, middle office, back office
- Function separation between trading and settlement
- Effective internal control system (ICS)
- Three lines of defense model

7. FIT AND PROPER REQUIREMENTS
All key persons must:
- Enjoy a good reputation (unbescholten)
- Have adequate professional qualifications
- Offer guarantee of proper business conduct (Gewaehr)
- Not have been subject to criminal conviction for financial crimes
- Submit FINMA personal questionnaires with supporting documentation

8. QUALIFIED PARTICIPATIONS
- Holders of 10%+ must be disclosed and approved by FINMA
- Beneficial ownership must be transparent through the chain
- Foreign controlling shareholders: reciprocity requirements may apply
- Material changes require prior FINMA approval

9. RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK
Securities firms must maintain:
- Market risk controls (VaR or standard approach)
- Credit and counterparty risk management
- Settlement and delivery risk controls
- Liquidity risk management with stress testing
- Operational risk management
- Legal and compliance risk assessment
- Position limits and stop-loss controls

10. CONDUCT RULES (FinSA)
Securities firms must comply with FinSA conduct requirements:
- Client classification (retail, professional, institutional)
- Suitability and appropriateness assessment
- Best execution policy
- Conflicts of interest management and disclosure
- Client information and documentation (KID)
- Record-keeping and order documentation

11. AML COMPLIANCE
Securities firms are financial intermediaries under AMLA and must:
- Implement comprehensive AML/KYC program
- Apply CDD and EDD procedures
- Implement transaction monitoring
- Sanctions screening (SECO, UN, EU lists)
- Market abuse prevention controls
- SAR reporting to MROS

12. TRADING AND SETTLEMENT
- Document trading systems and algorithms
- Implement pre- and post-trade controls
- Client asset segregation procedures
- Position reporting to trade repositories
- Margin and collateral management

13. INTERNAL CONTROLS
- Compliance function with adequate authority and resources
- Internal audit function with annual audit plan
- Risk management function independent from business lines
- Regular reporting to board and executive management

14. AUDIT REQUIREMENTS
- Licensing audit by FINMA-approved audit firm
- Annual regulatory audit
- Annual financial audit
- Special audits as directed by FINMA

15. APPLICATION PROCESS
- Submit application via FINMA EHP platform
- Processing time: typically 4-10 months
- Comprehensive documentation package required
- FINMA may conduct on-site visits during assessment
- License conditions may be imposed

16. ONGOING OBLIGATIONS
- Periodic regulatory reporting (capital, risk, financial)
- Report material organizational changes to FINMA
- Maintain minimum capital and own funds
- Annual regulatory and financial audits
- Compliance with FINMA circulars and guidance
