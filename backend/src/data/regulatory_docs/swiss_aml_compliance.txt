SWISS AML COMPLIANCE FOR FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS
=================================================

1. LEGAL FRAMEWORK
Swiss anti-money laundering (AML) regulation is based on:
- Federal Act on Combating Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing
  (AMLA, SR 955.0)
- Anti-Money Laundering Ordinance (AMLO, SR 955.01)
- FINMA Anti-Money Laundering Ordinance (AMLO-FINMA, SR 955.033.0)
- Agreement on the Swiss Banks' Code of Conduct with Regard to the
  Exercise of Due Diligence (CDB)
- Swiss Criminal Code Art. 260ter (criminal organization),
  Art. 305bis (money laundering), Art. 305ter (insufficient diligence)

2. SCOPE — FINANCIAL INTERMEDIARIES
The following are considered financial intermediaries under AMLA:
- Banks (BankA)
- Securities firms (FinIA)
- Fund management companies (CISA)
- Insurance companies offering life insurance (ISA)
- Payment service providers
- Money changers and money transmitters
- Persons who professionally accept, hold, or transfer assets

3. SRO MEMBERSHIP VS DIRECT FINMA SUPERVISION
Financial intermediaries must either:
- Join a self-regulatory organization (SRO) recognized by FINMA, OR
- Submit to direct FINMA AML supervision (FINMA SO)
SROs include: VQF, PolyReg, SO-FIT, ARIF, OAD FCT, and others.
Banks, securities firms, and fund management companies typically submit
to direct FINMA supervision for AML purposes.

4. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)
Financial intermediaries must verify:
- Identity of the contracting party (Art. 3 AMLA)
- Identity of the beneficial owner (Art. 4 AMLA)
- Purpose and nature of the business relationship (Art. 6 AMLA)
Identification documents required:
- Natural persons: valid ID document (passport, ID card)
- Legal entities: commercial register extract, articles of association
- Trusts: trust deed, identification of settlor and beneficiaries

5. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD)
Enhanced measures required for:
- Politically exposed persons (PEPs) — domestic and foreign
- Complex or unusual transactions
- Higher-risk countries (FATF grey/black list)
- Correspondent banking relationships
- Private banking and high-value transactions
- Legal structures with complex ownership chains

6. TRANSACTION MONITORING
Financial intermediaries must implement:
- Automated transaction monitoring systems
- Risk-based approach to monitoring thresholds
- Detection of unusual patterns and behaviors
- Cash transaction reporting above CHF 100,000 (for banks)
- Regular calibration and testing of monitoring rules

7. SANCTIONS SCREENING
Must screen against:
- SECO sanctions lists (Swiss primary lists)
- UN Security Council sanctions lists
- EU sanctions lists (commonly applied)
- OFAC SDN list (for USD-denominated transactions)
Screening required for:
- New client onboarding
- Ongoing monitoring of existing clients
- All payment transactions (sender and beneficiary)
- Real-time and batch screening capabilities

8. SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY REPORTING (SAR)
- Financial intermediaries must report to MROS (Money Laundering
  Reporting Office Switzerland) when:
  a) They know or have reasonable suspicion that assets are connected
     to money laundering, terrorist financing, or criminal organization
  b) Negotiations are broken off due to AML concerns
- Filing a SAR triggers a 5-working-day asset freeze
- MROS may extend the freeze to 20 working days
- Tipping-off prohibition applies

9. RECORD KEEPING
- All CDD documents must be retained for 10 years after the end of
  the business relationship (Art. 7 AMLA)
- Transaction records: 10 years
- Internal reports and risk assessments: 10 years
- SAR filings and related documentation: 10 years
- Records must be reproducible in Switzerland

10. RISK ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK
Financial intermediaries must conduct:
- Enterprise-wide AML risk assessment
- Client risk classification (low, medium, high, prohibited)
- Geographic risk assessment
- Product/service risk assessment
- Delivery channel risk assessment
- Regular updates (at least annually)

11. AML COMPLIANCE OFFICER
- Must appoint a qualified AML compliance officer
- Officer should have adequate authority, resources, and independence
- Responsibilities: AML program oversight, SAR filing, training,
  policy updates, regulatory reporting
- For banks and securities firms: preferably Swiss-resident
- Must have appropriate training and experience

12. TRAINING
- All employees must receive initial AML training
- Annual refresher training required
- Training must cover: CDD procedures, suspicious activity indicators,
  SAR filing process, sanctions requirements
- Records of training must be maintained

13. INTERNAL CONTROLS AND AUDIT
- Regular internal review of AML program effectiveness
- Independent testing of AML controls (internal audit or external)
- Board oversight of AML compliance
- Annual AML audit as part of regulatory audit

14. SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS BY INSTITUTION TYPE

Banks (BankA):
- Full AMLA, AMLO-FINMA, and CDB compliance
- Correspondent banking due diligence
- Wire transfer rules (FATF Recommendation 16)
- Cross-border payment transparency

Securities Firms (FinIA):
- AMLA and AMLO-FINMA compliance
- Market abuse prevention
- Transaction reporting to trade repositories
- Client asset protection

Fund Management Companies (CISA):
- AMLA compliance for fund subscriptions
- Investor identification and verification
- Source of funds documentation
- Distribution channel due diligence

Insurance Companies (ISA — Life Insurance):
- AMLA applies to life insurance activities
- Policyholder and beneficial owner identification
- Premium payment monitoring
- Surrender value monitoring

15. PENALTIES AND ENFORCEMENT
- Criminal penalties for money laundering: up to 5 years imprisonment
- Administrative penalties by FINMA: license revocation, industry bans
- SRO sanctions: fines, membership revocation
- Organizational deficiencies: FINMA may appoint an investigating agent
- Personal liability for responsible persons

16. RECENT DEVELOPMENTS
- Increased focus on beneficial ownership transparency
- Enhanced CDD for PEPs and high-risk jurisdictions
- Growing regulatory expectations for transaction monitoring
- FATF mutual evaluation recommendations being implemented
- Increasing coordination with EU AML framework developments
