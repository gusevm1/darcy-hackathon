BANKING ACT (BankA) — FULL BANKING LICENSE REQUIREMENTS
=========================================================

1. LEGAL BASIS
The Federal Act on Banks and Savings Banks (BankA, SR 952.0) and the
Banking Ordinance (BankO, SR 952.02) govern the licensing, organization,
and supervision of banks in Switzerland. Additional requirements come from
the Capital Adequacy Ordinance (CAO) and Liquidity Ordinance (LiqO).

2. DEFINITION OF BANKING ACTIVITY
A banking license is required for entities that:
- Accept deposits from the public on a professional basis, AND/OR
- Refinance themselves from multiple sources to provide financing, AND/OR
- Publicly advertise to accept deposits
(Art. 1 para. 2 BankA)

3. LEGAL FORM
Banks must be organized as:
- Corporation (AG) — most common
- Cooperative (Genossenschaft)
- Partnership (Kommanditgesellschaft) — rare
- Cantonal bank: public law entity
Foreign banks may operate through branches with FINMA approval.

4. MINIMUM CAPITAL
- Minimum paid-up capital: CHF 10 million (Art. 3 para. 2 let. b BankA)
- FINMA may require higher capital depending on:
  - Scope and complexity of activities
  - Risk profile
  - Balance sheet size
  - International activities

5. CAPITAL ADEQUACY (CAO — Basel III)
Banks must comply with Basel III capital requirements:
- Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1): minimum 4.5% of RWA
- Tier 1 capital: minimum 6% of RWA
- Total capital: minimum 8% of RWA
- Capital conservation buffer: 2.5%
- Countercyclical buffer: as set by SNB
- Leverage ratio: minimum 3%
Systemically important banks face additional requirements.

6. LIQUIDITY REQUIREMENTS (LiqO)
- Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR): minimum 100%
- Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR): minimum 100%
- Intraday liquidity management
- Liquidity stress testing
- Emergency liquidity planning

7. ORGANIZATIONAL REQUIREMENTS
- Board of directors (Verwaltungsrat):
  - Minimum 3 members, majority independent
  - Chairman cannot simultaneously be CEO
  - Audit committee, risk committee, compensation committee
  - Adequate expertise in banking, risk management, accounting
- Executive management (Geschaeftsleitung):
  - At least 2 qualified persons
  - Management conducted from Switzerland
  - Adequate professional qualifications and experience
- Three lines of defense model:
  - First line: business units (risk ownership)
  - Second line: risk management and compliance (oversight)
  - Third line: internal audit (independent assurance)

8. FIT AND PROPER REQUIREMENTS
All key persons must:
- Enjoy a good reputation (unbescholten)
- Have adequate professional qualifications
- Offer guarantee of proper business conduct (Gewaehr)
- Submit FINMA personal questionnaires
- Provide criminal record extracts, debt enforcement extracts
- Disclose all mandates and potential conflicts of interest

9. QUALIFIED PARTICIPATIONS
- 10%+ shareholders must be disclosed and approved
- Beneficial ownership chain must be transparent
- FINMA may deny if participation jeopardizes sound management
- Changes in qualified participations require prior FINMA approval

10. DEPOSIT PROTECTION
- Membership in esisuisse (Swiss deposit insurance scheme) mandatory
- Protected deposits: up to CHF 100,000 per depositor
- System-wide coverage limit: CHF 6 billion
- Contributions based on total privileged deposits

11. INTERNAL CONTROLS AND COMPLIANCE
- Comprehensive internal control system (ICS)
- Independent compliance function
- Risk management function with adequate authority
- Internal audit function with direct reporting to board
- Regular reporting on risk exposure and compliance status

12. AML COMPLIANCE
Banks must comply with comprehensive AML requirements:
- AMLA (Anti-Money Laundering Act)
- AMLO-FINMA (AML Ordinance)
- CDB (Agreement on Due Diligence by Banks)
- KYC/CDD/EDD procedures
- Transaction monitoring
- Sanctions screening (SECO, UN, EU, OFAC)
- PEP screening
- SAR reporting to MROS
- Record retention (10 years)

13. RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK
Comprehensive risk management covering:
- Credit risk (largest risk category for most banks)
- Market risk
- Interest rate risk in the banking book
- Operational risk
- Liquidity risk
- Concentration risk
- Reputational risk
- Strategic risk
- Legal and compliance risk

14. REPORTING OBLIGATIONS
- Quarterly: capital adequacy, large exposures, liquidity
- Semi-annually: balance sheet, income statement
- Annually: audited financial statements, annual report
- Ad hoc: material events, changes in organization or activities

15. AUDIT REQUIREMENTS
- Licensing audit (initial application)
- Annual regulatory audit by FINMA-recognized audit firm
- Annual financial audit
- Special audits as directed by FINMA

16. APPLICATION PROCESS
- Pre-notification to FINMA recommended
- Submit application via FINMA EHP platform
- Processing time: typically 6-18 months
- Comprehensive documentation package required
- FINMA may conduct on-site visits
- License may be subject to conditions and restrictions

17. ONGOING SUPERVISION
- Direct supervision by FINMA
- On-site inspections (planned and ad hoc)
- Thematic reviews
- Enforcement actions if non-compliant
- Recovery and resolution planning (for larger banks)
