1. LEGAL BASIS
The Federal Act on Collective Investment Schemes (CISA, SR 951.31) and the
Collective Investment Schemes Ordinance (CISO, SR 951.311) govern the
establishment and operation of fund management companies in Switzerland.

2. SCOPE
A fund management company (Fondsleitung) manages Swiss collective investment
schemes (contractual funds) on behalf of investors. It acts in its own name
but for the account of the fund investors.

3. LICENSING REQUIREMENT
Any entity managing Swiss collective investment schemes requires a FINMA
license as a fund management company (Art. 28 CISA). Operating without a
license is a criminal offence.

4. LEGAL FORM
Fund management companies must be organized as a corporation (AG) or limited
partnership with shares (Kommandit-AG) under Swiss law (Art. 28 para. 2 CISA).

5. MINIMUM CAPITAL
- Minimum share capital: CHF 1 million, fully paid-up (Art. 29 CISA)
- Own funds must be maintained at adequate levels considering the volume
  and complexity of managed funds
- FINMA may require additional own funds based on risk assessment

6. ORGANIZATIONAL REQUIREMENTS
- Board of directors with at least three members
- Majority of board members must be independent
- Executive management must have adequate professional qualifications
  and experience in fund management
- Clear separation of functions: portfolio management, risk management,
  compliance, and operations
- Effective internal control system (ICS)
- Independent compliance and risk management functions

7. FIT AND PROPER REQUIREMENTS
All persons responsible for management and business conduct must:
- Enjoy a good reputation
- Have adequate professional qualifications
- Offer guarantee of proper business conduct (Gewaehr)
- Submit FINMA personal questionnaires
- Provide criminal record and debt enforcement extracts

8. QUALIFIED PARTICIPATIONS
Holders of qualified participations (10% or more) must:
- Be identified and disclosed to FINMA
- Not jeopardize sound and prudent management
- Obtain FINMA approval before acquiring qualified participations

9. FUND MANAGEMENT AGREEMENT (FONDSVERTRAG)
- Must be approved by FINMA before fund launch
- Contains investment policy, fee structure, valuation rules
- Defines rights and obligations of fund management company,
  custodian bank, and investors

10. CUSTODIAN BANK (DEPOTBANK)
- Mandatory appointment of a custodian bank (Art. 72 CISA)
- Custodian bank must be a Swiss-licensed bank
- Responsibilities: safekeeping of fund assets, payment transactions,
  supervision of fund management company
- Custodian bank must be independent from the fund management company

11. INVESTOR PROTECTION
- Duty to act in the best interests of investors
- Equal treatment of investors
- Transparent fee disclosure
- Regular reporting (semi-annual and annual reports)
- Right of investors to redeem units at NAV

12. RISK MANAGEMENT
- Comprehensive risk management framework
- Liquidity management with stress testing
- Counterparty risk monitoring
- Market risk controls
- Operational risk management

13. AML COMPLIANCE
Fund management companies are financial intermediaries under AMLA and must:
- Implement AML/KYC procedures
- Affiliate with an SRO or submit to direct FINMA AML supervision
- Comply with the CDB (Agreement on Due Diligence)

14. AUDIT REQUIREMENTS
- Annual financial audit by a FINMA-recognized audit firm
- Regulatory audit covering compliance with CISA requirements
- Fund-level audits for each managed collective investment scheme

15. APPLICATION PROCESS
- Submit application via FINMA EHP platform
- Processing time: typically 4-10 months
- Application fee and annual supervisory levy apply
- FINMA may impose conditions on the license

16. ONGOING OBLIGATIONS
- Report material changes to FINMA
- Maintain minimum capital and own funds requirements
- Submit periodic regulatory reports
- Comply with FINMA circulars and guidance
