POLYREG MEMBERSHIP RULES AND REQUIREMENTS
PolyReg General Self-Regulatory Organisation
Comprehensive Guide to PolyReg Affiliation for Financial Intermediaries

================================================================================
1. POLYREG BACKGROUND
================================================================================

1.1 Overview

PolyReg (PolyReg General Self-Regulatory Organisation, also known as PolyReg
Allg. Selbstregulierungsorganisation) is a FINMA-recognized self-regulatory
organization (SRO) under Art. 24 of the Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA,
SR 955.0). Established in 2000, PolyReg has positioned itself as a forward-
looking SRO with a particular focus on fintech, innovation, and technology-
driven financial services.

Key facts:
- Headquarters: Zurich, Switzerland
- FINMA recognition: Since 2000
- Focus areas: Fintech, crypto/blockchain, payment services, digital assets
- Languages: German, English, French
- Governance: Board, Management, Admission Committee, Disciplinary Committee
- Member base: Smaller than VQF, more specialized and curated

1.2 Fintech and Crypto Specialization

PolyReg has cultivated a reputation as the SRO of choice for fintech startups,
blockchain companies, and innovative financial service providers. Key
distinguishing features:

- Deep understanding of crypto/blockchain business models
- Willingness to engage with novel structures and technologies
- Practical, solution-oriented approach to compliance challenges
- Faster, more personalized admission process for well-prepared applicants
- Direct engagement between PolyReg leadership and applicants
- Active participation in Swiss fintech and crypto industry discussions

1.3 Regulatory Framework

PolyReg's regulatory documents include:
- PolyReg Regulations (Reglement): The primary rulebook implementing AMLA
  due diligence obligations
- PolyReg Statutes: Governance and organizational rules
- PolyReg Directives: Supplementary guidance documents
- PolyReg Circulars: Periodic updates and guidance letters

All PolyReg regulations are subject to FINMA approval and must be consistent
with AMLA, AMLO, and AMLO-FINMA.

2. MEMBERSHIP ELIGIBILITY
================================================================================

2.1 Who Can Join PolyReg

PolyReg admits financial intermediaries under Art. 2 para. 3 AMLA, including:

- Crypto and virtual asset businesses:
  - Crypto exchanges (fiat-to-crypto, crypto-to-fiat, crypto-to-crypto)
  - Crypto brokers and OTC trading desks
  - Crypto custody/wallet providers (within regulatory thresholds)
  - Token issuers (where the issuance constitutes financial intermediation)
  - Crypto payment processors
  - Staking and yield service providers
  - NFT platforms (where AML obligations apply)

- Traditional financial intermediaries:
  - Payment service providers
  - Money transmitters
  - Currency exchange operators
  - Asset managers (non-CISA)
  - Trust and fiduciary service providers
  - Investment advisors with disposition powers
  - Lending platforms (peer-to-peer, crowdlending)

- Fintech companies:
  - Digital payment platforms
  - Robo-advisors
  - Crowdfunding platforms
  - RegTech and compliance technology providers (where they handle client assets)

2.2 Who Cannot Join

PolyReg cannot admit entities that require a FINMA prudential license:
- Banks and savings institutions (Banking Act)
- Securities firms (FinIA)
- DLT trading facilities (FMIA)
- FinTech license holders (Art. 1b Banking Act)
- Fund management companies (CISA)

These entities are directly supervised by FINMA for AML purposes.

2.3 Swiss Nexus Requirement

Applicants must have a genuine connection to Switzerland:
- Swiss-incorporated legal entity, or
- Swiss branch of a foreign entity registered in the commercial register
- Genuine Swiss substance (office, staff, operational activities)
- At least one Swiss-resident member of management

3. APPLICATION PROCESS
================================================================================

3.1 Phase 1: Initial Engagement (Week 1–2)

(a) Contact PolyReg to discuss the planned application
    - PolyReg is accessible and typically responds within a few business days
    - An initial call or meeting is arranged to discuss the business model
    - PolyReg provides guidance on eligibility, documentation requirements,
      and expected timeline

(b) Preliminary Assessment
    - PolyReg may provide a preliminary view on whether the business model
      falls within AMLA scope and is suitable for SRO membership
    - This is not a binding opinion but helps applicants prepare

3.2 Phase 2: Application Preparation (Weeks 2–6)

(a) Prepare all required documentation (see Section 4)
(b) Develop or finalize the AML compliance framework
(c) Complete the PolyReg application form
(d) Internal review and quality check of submission materials

Tip: PolyReg appreciates well-prepared applications. Investing time in this
phase significantly reduces the review period.

3.3 Phase 3: Formal Submission (Week 6)

(a) Submit the complete application package to PolyReg
(b) Pay the application processing fee
(c) PolyReg acknowledges receipt and confirms completeness

3.4 Phase 4: Review (Weeks 6–12)

(a) PolyReg conducts a thorough review of the application:
    - Corporate structure and beneficial ownership
    - Fit and proper assessment of key personnel
    - Business model and risk analysis
    - AML compliance framework adequacy
    - Technical infrastructure (particularly for crypto businesses)
    - Financial viability

(b) Queries and dialogue:
    - PolyReg will raise questions and request clarifications
    - Applicants should respond promptly to avoid delays
    - PolyReg may suggest modifications to the AML framework

(c) Interview or on-site visit:
    - For crypto businesses, PolyReg typically conducts an interview with
      management and the compliance officer
    - On-site visits may be conducted for more complex applications

3.5 Phase 5: Decision (Weeks 10–14)

(a) The Admission Committee reviews the application and issues a decision:
    - Approval: Membership is granted
    - Conditional approval: Specific conditions must be met within a deadline
    - Rejection: Detailed reasons provided

(b) Typical timeline: 2–4 months from complete submission to decision
    - Well-prepared applications: 2–3 months
    - Applications requiring significant remediation: 3–6 months

3.6 Phase 6: Activation

(a) Sign the membership agreement
(b) Pay admission fee and annual membership fee
(c) PolyReg registers the member with FINMA
(d) Member may commence operations as a registered financial intermediary

4. REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION
================================================================================

4.1 Corporate and Legal Documents

- Completed PolyReg application form
- Articles of incorporation or partnership agreement
- Commercial register extract (not older than 3 months)
- Organizational chart
- Shareholder register
- Beneficial ownership structure and declaration
- Board resolution authorizing the application
- Sample contracts / terms of service for clients

4.2 Key Personnel Documents

For all directors, management members, AML compliance officer, and beneficial
owners (25%+):
- Curriculum vitae
- Copy of valid passport or identity card
- Swiss criminal record extract (Strafregisterauszug), not older than 3 months
- Debt enforcement register extract (Betreibungsregisterauszug), not older
  than 3 months
- Professional certifications and qualifications
- Self-declaration / fit and proper questionnaire
- For foreign nationals: criminal record extract from country of residence

4.3 Business Documentation

- Business plan including:
  - Detailed description of services and products
  - Target market and client types
  - Geographic scope of operations
  - Revenue model and financial projections (3 years)
  - Competitive positioning
  - Marketing and distribution channels
  - For crypto: technical architecture, blockchain(s) used, token types
    supported, custody solution details
- IT security concept (particularly important for crypto businesses)
- Business continuity plan
- Insurance policies (professional liability, cyber insurance where applicable)
- Bank account confirmation (Swiss bank)

4.4 AML Compliance Framework

- AML/CFT policy manual tailored to the specific business
- Client onboarding and KYC procedures
- Risk classification methodology
- Enhanced due diligence procedures
- Transaction monitoring procedures and systems
- SAR filing procedures
- Sanctions screening procedures
- Travel rule compliance procedures (for VASPs)
- Record-keeping procedures
- Compliance officer appointment and job description
- Training plan and schedule
- Internal audit framework

5. FEES
================================================================================

5.1 Application Processing Fee
- Approximately CHF 2,000 – 4,000 (non-refundable)
- Covers the review process

5.2 Admission Fee (One-Time)
- Approximately CHF 2,000 – 8,000
- Depends on the complexity and size of the business
- Payable upon approval of membership

5.3 Annual Membership Fee
- Base component: approximately CHF 1,500 – 3,000
- Variable component based on business volume, number of clients, or risk level
- Typical total: CHF 2,000 – 10,000 per year
- Invoiced annually

5.4 External Audit Costs (Separate)
- CHF 5,000 – 25,000+ per year
- Paid directly to the PolyReg-approved audit firm
- Depends on the size and complexity of the business

5.5 Other Costs
- Training events: nominal fees
- Late payment surcharges per PolyReg fee schedule
- Extraordinary audit costs (if triggered by compliance issues)

Note: PolyReg's fee structure is generally competitive, particularly for
smaller fintech and crypto startups.

6. ONGOING OBLIGATIONS
================================================================================

6.1 AML Compliance

Members must maintain full compliance with PolyReg's Regulations, which
implement the AMLA due diligence obligations:
- Client identification and verification (KYC)
- Beneficial ownership determination
- Risk-based client classification
- Enhanced due diligence for high-risk relationships
- Ongoing monitoring of business relationships and transactions
- Transaction monitoring (manual and/or automated)
- SAR filing with MROS
- Sanctions screening
- Travel rule compliance (for VASPs)
- Record-keeping (10 years minimum)

6.2 Annual Reporting

Members must submit annual reports to PolyReg covering:
- Business activities during the year
- Number of clients and transactions
- Compliance activities (SARs filed, sanctions hits, EDD cases)
- Changes to the business model, personnel, or structure
- Training conducted
- Compliance officer's assessment

6.3 External AML Audit

- Frequency: annually for higher-risk members, biennially for lower-risk members
- Audit firm: must be approved by PolyReg
- Scope: comprehensive review of AML compliance
- Audit report: submitted to PolyReg within the prescribed deadline
- Remediation: members must address findings within deadlines

6.4 Notification of Material Changes

Members must promptly notify PolyReg of:
- Changes to directors, management, or compliance officer
- Changes in beneficial ownership
- Material changes to business model or product offerings
- Criminal proceedings against key personnel
- Regulatory actions or investigations
- Cybersecurity incidents or significant operational disruptions
- Planned mergers, acquisitions, or liquidation

6.5 Training

- AML compliance officer: ongoing professional development
- All relevant staff: AML training at least annually
- Records of all training must be maintained

7. AML REQUIREMENTS — DETAILED
================================================================================

7.1 Client Identification

PolyReg's regulations require identification of the contracting party in
accordance with AMLA Art. 3:

Natural persons:
- Full name, date of birth, nationality, residential address
- Verification by valid identity document
- Methods: face-to-face, video identification, online identification,
  certified copy

Legal entities:
- Company name, registered office, commercial register number
- Commercial register extract
- Identification of authorized representatives
- Beneficial ownership structure

7.2 Beneficial Ownership

- Written BO declaration required for all business relationships
- BO = natural person(s) with ultimate control (typically 25%+ ownership)
- For complex structures: trace through to ultimate beneficial owners
- Verification of plausibility required

7.3 Risk Assessment

PolyReg requires a documented risk assessment methodology covering:
- Client risk factors (PEPs, geographic risk, industry risk, etc.)
- Product/service risk factors
- Delivery channel risk factors (non-face-to-face, online)
- Geographic risk factors
- Transaction risk factors

Risk levels: low, medium, high, unacceptable (decline relationship)

7.4 Transaction Monitoring

- Proportionate to the size and complexity of the business
- Smaller businesses: manual monitoring may be acceptable
- Larger businesses: automated monitoring systems required
- For crypto businesses: blockchain analytics tools required
- Red flags specific to crypto: mixing services, privacy coin transactions,
  rapid movement through multiple wallets, disproportionate transactions

7.5 Sanctions Screening

- Screen against SECO sanctions lists (Swiss implementation of UN sanctions)
- Additional screening against EU, OFAC, and other relevant lists
- Real-time screening for new clients
- Ongoing screening upon list updates
- For crypto: wallet address screening against known sanctioned addresses

8. SPECIFIC PROVISIONS FOR CRYPTO/BLOCKCHAIN COMPANIES
================================================================================

8.1 Crypto Business Categories

PolyReg has developed specific guidance for different crypto business types:

(a) Crypto Exchanges: Must implement full AML compliance including blockchain
    analytics, travel rule, and robust client verification.

(b) Crypto Brokers/OTC: Similar to exchanges but may have additional
    requirements for large-value transactions.

(c) Custody Providers: Must demonstrate secure key management, segregation of
    client assets, backup procedures, and operational resilience.

(d) Token Issuers: Must classify tokens, apply appropriate due diligence to
    investors, and ensure ongoing compliance for secondary market activities.

(e) Payment Processors: Must comply with travel rule and implement transaction
    monitoring appropriate for payment flows.

8.2 Technical Requirements for Crypto Members

- Blockchain analytics: Must use at least one recognized blockchain analytics
  tool (e.g., Chainalysis, Elliptic, Crystal, Scorechain)
- Wallet management: Documented procedures for hot/cold wallets, key management,
  and access controls
- Travel rule: Technical implementation using TRISA, OpenVASP, or equivalent
- Cybersecurity: Documented IT security concept, incident response plan,
  penetration testing
- Business continuity: Disaster recovery plan covering key loss scenarios

8.3 Supported Token Types

PolyReg members may deal in various token types, subject to risk assessment:
- Bitcoin (BTC) and major cryptocurrencies: Standard risk
- Stablecoins (USDT, USDC, etc.): Standard risk with issuer assessment
- Privacy coins (Monero, Zcash): Enhanced due diligence required; some
  members restrict or prohibit
- DeFi tokens: Case-by-case risk assessment
- NFTs: Assessment of AML applicability required

9.1 When to Choose PolyReg Over VQF

Choose PolyReg if:
- You are a fintech startup seeking a streamlined admission process
- Your business model is innovative and may require flexible regulatory dialogue
- You value direct access to SRO leadership
- You want a crypto-specialized SRO
- You are a smaller operation and want personalized service

Choose VQF if:
- You want the most established and largest SRO
- You need multi-language support (especially French and Italian)
- You have a more traditional financial intermediary business
- You want the broadest network of member firms
- Brand recognition of SRO membership is important

10. ADVANTAGES FOR FINTECH STARTUPS
================================================================================

10.1 Practical Benefits

(a) Speed: PolyReg can often process well-prepared applications faster than
    larger SROs, which is critical for startups with time pressure.

(b) Flexibility: PolyReg is willing to engage with novel business models and
    work collaboratively with applicants to find compliant solutions.

(c) Cost efficiency: PolyReg's fee structure is competitive, which is important
    for early-stage companies with limited budgets.

(d) Knowledge sharing: PolyReg's concentration of fintech and crypto members
    creates a knowledge network that benefits all members.

(e) Regulatory bridge: For startups planning to eventually obtain a FINMA
    license, PolyReg membership provides a compliant starting point while the
    business scales.

10.2 Common Startup Scenarios

Scenario 1: Crypto exchange startup
- Start with PolyReg SRO membership
- Operate within FINMA sandbox thresholds (< 20 clients, < CHF 1M deposits)
- Scale up and apply for FinTech license when thresholds are exceeded

Scenario 2: Blockchain payment processor
- PolyReg SRO membership for AML compliance
- Implement travel rule from day one
- Maintain robust transaction monitoring

Scenario 3: Tokenization platform
- PolyReg SRO membership for the intermediary function
- Token classification analysis with PolyReg input
- Potential additional FINMA licensing if tokens qualify as securities

11. DISCIPLINARY PROCEEDINGS
================================================================================

11.1 Grounds

PolyReg may initiate disciplinary proceedings for:
- Violation of PolyReg Regulations, AMLA, or AMLO
- Failure to remediate audit findings
- Failure to file SARs
- Providing false information
- Non-payment of fees
- Non-cooperation with PolyReg
- Reputational risk to PolyReg or the SRO system

11.2 Sanctions

- Written warning
- Reprimand
- Fine (up to CHF 500,000)
- Conditions or restrictions on membership
- Suspension
- Expulsion

11.3 Procedure

- Investigation by PolyReg
- Notification of charges
- Right to be heard
- Decision by Disciplinary Committee
- Right of appeal
- Serious violations reported to FINMA
